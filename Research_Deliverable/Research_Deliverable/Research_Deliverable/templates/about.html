Disability in the Digital World: How Representation Varies Across Cultures and Languages

Abstract

  • This prototype is for the page that contains the line graph that will outline the overall change in number of bites every year for each borough. This graph allows the viewer to compare the boroughs to each other and the progression of the amount of bites in one single borough.
  • Feedback: Some of the feedback that I have recieved for this prototype is to create more tick marks so that the viewer can read the points on the line graph better. Additionally, someone recommended that I should delete the end tags with the boroughs and just have a legend in order to make more room for the unit tick marks to not overwhelm the viewer. Lastly, I was recommended to change some of the colors, specifically red and green, so that the line graph and the ability to compare lines is accessible to all.
  • Takeaway: It is interesting to see all of the boroughs during the year 2015-2023 as a comparison. I initially predicted that Staten Island would have less dog bites than the other boroughs; however, it was fascinating to see the values of all the other boroughs and how Manhattan had the most reported male dog bites almost every year.
  • This prototype is for the specific yearly pages that will show a map of the 5 boroughs of NYC in a given year. All values, which are the numbers dog bites in each borough, are represented with color. This allows for the viewer to compare the values for each borough in one specific year.
  • Feedback: Some of the feedback that I recieved for this prototype is to make sure to isolate the NYC boroughs and delete New Jersey and the Airports. This is to concentrate all the data points and make it more accessible to the viewer by not overwhelming them.
  • Takeaway: I found it to be easiest to demonstrate the number of dog bites each year from each borough in a map and be able to compare it through color as well as through numbers. I think that this makes it more accessible for more people because they would not need to compare numbers, but just different shadings of blue.
